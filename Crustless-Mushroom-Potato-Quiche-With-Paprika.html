Crustless Mushroom, Potato Quiche With Paprika

We are obsessed with paprika in my house. In fact, we keep a giant container on hand because we go through it so fast. This easy weeknight dinner takes it to the next level by pairing a smoked Spanish paprika with mushrooms and potatoes. It's a match made in heaven.

INGREDIENTS

  • Olive oil, 1 tablespoon
  • Yellow onion, 1 small
  • White mushrooms, 8-ounce package
  • Yukon gold potatoes, 2 medium
  • Salt, 1 tablespoon
  • Paprika (smoked Spanish style, if available), 1 teaspoon
  • Eggs, 6
  • Dijon mustard, 1 tablespoon
  • Fat-free Ricotta cheese, half cup
  • Almond milk, half cup
  • Part-skim Italian blend cheese, half cup

Spray pie pan with cooking spray. Set aside. Preheat oven to 375 degrees F.

Heat saute pan on medium heat. I prefer to use a cast iron skillet. Heat oil. Dice onion and add to pan. Cook 3-5 minutes, until starting to lightly brown.

Chop mushrooms and add to skillet. Cook for 3 more minutes.

Dice potatoes and add to skillet. Cook for 10 minutes or so, until softened and any liquid in pan is absorbed. Add salt and paprika. Cook for 2 more minutes. Remove from heat and add mixture to pie pan to cool.

While the veggies are cooling, combine eggs, ricotta, mustard and almond milk. Mix well. Pour egg mixture over vegetables in pie pan. Sprinkle Italian cheese evenly over the top. Cook until the top is puffed and golden brown, and knife inserted in the center comes out clean, about 45 minutes.

Serves 4. Nutrition information per serving: Calories, 250; total fat, 15 grams; saturated fat, 5.5 grams; total carbohydrates, 13.2 grams, fiber, 1.5g; total sugars, 2.4 grams; protein 16.7 grams.
